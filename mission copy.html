Chandrayaan 2 was a lunar exploration mission launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in July 2019. It was India's second mission to the Moon, following the successful Chandrayaan 1 mission in 2008. The primary objective of Chandrayaan 2 was to land a rover on the lunar surface and study the Moon's south polar region, which had not been explored extensively before. The mission consisted of three main components: the Orbiter, the Vikram lander, and the Pragyan rover.
The Orbiter, which remained in orbit around the Moon, carried a suite of scientific instruments to study the lunar surface, atmosphere, and exosphere.
